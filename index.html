About

“Fabrication of an autonomous modular ROV for analysing and monitoring the condition of water bodies of the villages located in Budhera, Haryana.”

Problem statement

"There is a need of a system capable of analysing and monitoring the water conditions of all the water bodies located near the villages present near SGT University campus which is located in Haryana, India.

The Budhera village has population of 1575 of which 837 are males while 738 are females as per Population Census 2011 [1] therefore it becomes very necessary to inspect and monitor the quality of water that they are getting from these water sources, be it be for running some of the mundane works and chores. Moreover, village people have cows and buffaloes to raise and in order to clean their cattle they often use these static water resources like the Budhera Pond. Since some of the water bodies have become polluted or too contaminated for anyone's use, it becomes propitious to check the hydrochemistry of the water as well as monitor the depth of such bodies as far as environment and human health is concerned."

Solution

" We are proposing an autonomous modular boat system which will be easy to upgrade as everything that will be implemented will be in a different module that will make it easy to run the different operations using one boat with ease.

We are proposing fabrication of the stated system into different modules:

  • Module 1

    • Depth measuring module, which will help us in calculating a significant amount of volumetric holdings that will help in understanding the amount of water that is wasted or polluted.

  • Module 2

    • Sensor assembly module, which will help us in analysing the condition of the water by finding out the TDS, Turbidity, pH and Dissolved Oxygen to see to what extent has the water become polluted. Or each parameter there are individual sensors that will be used in order to achieve this goal.

    • TDS, Total dissolved solids (TDS) refer to the amount of minerals, metals, organic material and salts that are dissolved in a certain water volume that is expressed in mg/L. It is directly associated with the quality and purity of water, particularly in water purification systems.
    • Turbidity is a measure of the cloudiness of water, caused predominantly by suspended materials such as clay, silt, organic and inorganic matter, plankton and other microscopic organisms, and scattering and absorbing light.
    • pH, is defined as the negative logarithm of H+ ion concentration. Hence, the meaning of the name pH is justifying the power of the hydrogen. There is a scale for pH which tells the characteristics of water in terms of their acidic or basic nature.
    • Dissolved Oxygen, is the amount of the oxygen present in the water bodies, this is the oxygen which are required by the aquatic flora and fauna to survive and grow.
  • Module 3

    • Boat assembly, that will keep all the motors, microcontrollers, Battery pack and electronics inside one big casing that will allow good waterproofing upto a certain extent.

  • Module 4

    • Remote sensing module, for manoeuvring the stated ROV with ease using the mobile app or a remote.

Prior Research

There are many research papers published which state the use of ROVs for imaging, mapping and monitoring water pollution. In a paper published by Mohamad Adhipramana, Rina Mardiati and Edi Mulyana from Bandung, Indonesia emphasized on making a ROV robot for monitoring the quality of water based on IoT.

Their research methodologies involved:

  1. Designing of the robotic system.
  2. Designing of monitoring systems.
  3. Designing software for the same

Their ROV had a speed of 0.46 km per hour and the distance it can reach was upto 140 meters. Their ROV was limited to providing data of pH, NTU, & PPM [1]. In the other paper by Liah X. Coggins and Anas Ghadoani, from University of western Australia, Crawley, Australia stated high resolution bathymetric mapping. From their paper it can be clearly seen that using ROV for any bathymetric survey or even monitoring purpose is a very good and reliable choice [2]. Their ROV had Sonar with a GPS module which made the ROV autonomous. We also got a lot of insights on how to make our prototype by reading a research article which stated the use of Arduino Uno to find out water turbidity measurement devices. In this paper Researchers have developed a turbidity measuring device using a microcontroller essentially Arduino Uno and a photoresistor sensor [3]. Going through these papers gave us the idea that the direction we are going in is the right one and there are a lot of resource’s online to make this project into a reality for the greater good of humanity. We also did some prior research on locating different water bodies and even visited Budhera Pond located in Budhera, Haryana, India

USP

In developing such a system, we are solving the issues of absence of data of such water bodies since they are small but can affect the areas growth and health conditions of the people residing there.

Main benefit of the developing such system is to analyse and monitor the water quality and gather data to get a know-how about the place's water conditions that might have been affecting the lies of the people in a negative cartesian on a longer run or we can say it might have been leading them in increased Biomagnifications. We will be able to study and pitch a restoration plan of such waterbodies in future for a greater good of flora and fauna and humans.

This system will help in generating the action plan for revival of such water bodies in the long run affecting many lives.

The coupling of depth measuring module with the ROV platform has several advantages over traditional measurement techniques:

  1. Requires less manual labour.
  2. Reduce risks and safety equipment requirements, as we can control the ROV using the remote or laptop
  3. Reduce the human error in reading and the difference in having different people conduct these types of surveys.
  4. It will collect many more data points as compared to a man on a boat performing the same depth measurement technique

The coupling of all the modules together has certain advantages as compared to physically going on a boat and taking samples of water from different spots like:

  1. Automatic updation of the reading onto the SD Card.
  2. We can pre determine the trajectory of the boat using GPS points or by pre-calculating the points using simulation software
  3. Can change survey time based on the requirement of no. of data points
  4. Less to no Human error in readings as the readings will be acquired by the sensors
  5. We can divide the operation area by dividing the selected water body into quadrants for ROV to survey more precisely.
